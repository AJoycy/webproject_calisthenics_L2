Push Exercises

Push exercises often develop chest, shoulders and triceps. They are essential for movements relying on pushing your body away from an object.

Push-ups

Execution

Plank position on the ground. Hands at chest level, shoulder width and elbows close to body. Lower yourself straight down, push back up in a controlled movement.

Common Mistakes

  • Flaring elbows outside.
  • Rushing to get down.
  • Half-reps.

Variations

  • Decline push-ups: elevated feet (harder)
  • Incline push-ups: elevated hands (easier)

Pseudo Push-up

Execution

Place your body in a plank position, hands below shoulders like a regular Push-up. Now move your hands at belly button level, and lower then push yourself up in a vertical motion.

Common Mistakes

  • Flaring elbows outside.
  • Not engaging glutes and core.
  • Hips sagging.

Pike Push-ups

Execution

Start the same way as a regular push-up, but hands placed significantly closer to your feet, making your body do a V shape. Start then to lowering yourself while adding a slight forward movement.

Common Mistakes

  • Flaring elbows outside.
  • Using ankles as pivot (whole leg should stay stiff).
  • Lacking range of motion (should be head to ground - full arm extension).

Dips

Execution

Hang between two parallel bars with straight arms. Lower until chest reaches bar levels.

Common Mistakes

  • Excessive forward lean.
  • No full arm extension.
  • Wrists not locked.

Tuck Planche Push-ups

Execution

Start as if preparing for dips, but stick your knees to your chest while making your upper body parallel to ground. Then push down and up in a standard push-up way.

Common Mistakes

  • Not using lower body muscles.
  • Not locking out arms and pushing out scapulas at extension.

Variations

  • Advanced: extend your hips more, knees perpendicular with floor
  • Straddle: fully extended legs but spread
  • Full planche: fully extended straight legs

Handstand Push-ups

Execution

Lower yourself in a controlled way slightly forward in a handstand position. Push back up to start position.

Common Mistakes

  • Not going all the way down.
  • Neglecting balance.
  • Rounded banana body.

Variations

  • Wall assisted

Pull Exercises

Pull as it name says focuses on pulling your body towards objects and resisting other forces (often gravity). They are required to gain stability, back and grip strength.

Pull-ups

Execution

Start with a dead hang on the bar, pull yourself up to chest level. Prioritise power on the pull, control the descent on arm extensions. Slightly round your back to get a hollow body.

Common Mistakes

  • Rushing reps.
  • Swinging to get momementum.

Variations

  • Australian pull-ups: Your feet rest on the floor on a lower bar, horizontal body.
  • Negative: Doing only the descending phase of the movement (easier)

Chin-ups

Execution

Same start as pullup, but palms face up (supination). Pull up straight, focus on biceps contraction, chest to bar.

Common Mistakes

  • Focusing away from biceps contraction.
  • No full arm lockout.

Variations

  • Same as pull-ups

Archer Pull-ups

Execution

Start hanging to bar with a much wider grip than shoulder width. Now pull towards one side with an arm, using the other as assistance. Keep the passive arm straight the whole time, chest to bar.

Common Mistakes

  • Bending passive arm.
  • Not going back to start dead hang position at the bottom
  • Lacking range of motion

One arm pulll-ups

Execution

Dead hang with a single arm. Pull yourself up with raw power. Full range of motion, no swinging. Requires to be able to pull at least twice your body weight up.

Common Mistakes

  • Starting without having enough strength
  • Bad warm-up
  • Swinging

Muscle-ups

Execution

Same as a pullup, but with a twist. Pull your body around the bar instead of towards it. Think about smashing the bar on the floor instead of pulling it.

Common Mistakes

  • Swinging
  • Not rounding enough to pass the bar

Variations

  • Chin-ups: take supinated hands placement

Core Exercises

Your core is what makes your movements stable and controlled. Everything goes through your core when you move. A weak core means weak contractions, a strong core translates to an easier time mastering advanced skills.

Banana Holds

Execution

Start laying on your back, then lift up arms and legs to take a banana shape. Push your pelvis forward (anterior pelvic tilt). Stay as immobile as possible.

Common Mistakes

  • Lower back not touching the floor

Variations

  • Bent legs (as seen on video)
  • Straightening legs separately (as on video)

Core compression

Execution

Start sitting on the floor with a straight back. Place hands slightly before knee level. Now lift your legs and push the floor with your hands. Focus on abdominal and hip flexor contractions. Keep knees straight.

Common Mistakes

  • Rushing reps (stay slow)

Variations

  • Single leg lift

Leg raises

Execution

Hang on a pull-up bar or between parallel bars in a dips position. Push the bar away from you if on parallel bars. Slowly lift straight legs as high as you can, descend as slow as possible, keep a slight hip angle at the bottom instead of coming back straight down.

Common Mistakes

  • Body swings

Variations

  • Single legged
  • Bent knees

L-Sit

Execution

Take a dip position between two surfaces. Push shoulders down, extend toes and brace your belly. Lift your legs in a L shape, hold until you can't anymore.

Common Mistakes

  • Saggy shoulders

L-sit to tuck

Execution

Same steps as an L-sit, but now we add dynamic movements. Send legs backward in a controlled way, bend knees toward chest into a Tuck-planche position, then go back to the starting L-sit.

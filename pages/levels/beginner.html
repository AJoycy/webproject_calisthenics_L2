Recommended Beginner Milestones

These are some common skills beginners should aim to achieve before moving to the intermediate level.

L-Sit

Develops core strength, hip flexibility and body control.

Handstand

Greater balance, shoulder stability, spacial awareness.

Pull-ups

Builds baseline pulling power, develops your back, prepares for future more incredible skills.

Training Guidelines

Proper Form

Form > Reps | The quality of the movements matters more than their quantity.

Consistency

Consistency > Occasional limit-pushing. Doing regular training will take you further than pushing very hard once in a while.

Recovery

Take rest! Rest is as important as training. Your body can only get stronger during your rest. Neglected recovery will ineluctably lead to injury and progress stalling.

Progressive Overload

You need to periodically challenge yourself and move up in training intensity/difficulty. Staying in the comfort zone will only keep you where you are.

Beginner Tip

Do not rush! Foundations are the most important, you can not progress without mastering them first. Celebrate small improvements, and take patience - it is going to be long :)
